About This Dictionary

This digital dictionary offers a structured, analytically grounded lexicon for scholars, practitioners, and curious readers engaging with Indonesian gastrodiplomacy. Gastrodiplomacy — the strategic use of culinary culture to advance national identity, foreign policy goals, and cultural outreach — has emerged as a significant dimension of Indonesia's international engagement. This dictionary maps its key concepts, agents, practices, institutions, and food objects, situating each within the broader field of cultural diplomacy and transnational food studies. Entries are organized thematically and alphabetically, combining definitional precision with contextual analysis. The dictionary draws on frameworks from cultural studies, political science, food anthropology, and international relations, offering an interdisciplinary portal into one of Southeast Asia's most gastronomically rich nations and its global culinary projection. It is designed as a living reference — expandable, revisable, and open to emerging scholarship.
